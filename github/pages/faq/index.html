Common questions

Answers people look for before they apply.

If you are considering pre-settlement funding, the first step is understanding what it is, how the review process works, and what to ask before signing anything.

What is pre-settlement funding?

Pre-settlement funding is money advanced against a potential future settlement. It is meant to help people manage day-to-day expenses while a case is still pending.

Is it the same as a traditional loan?

Many people use the term “lawsuit loan,” but agreements can work differently from a standard personal loan. Terms, fees, and repayment conditions should always be reviewed carefully.

Who may qualify?

Funding is generally considered for people with an active case and an attorney who can provide the details needed for review. Whether funding is available depends on the specific facts of the case.

What information is usually needed?

The review usually starts with your contact information, the type of case involved, and the name of the attorney or firm handling it.

Why does attorney coordination matter?

Funding decisions are typically based on case information, so the process often involves coordinating with your attorney to review status, documentation, and other relevant details.

What can funds usually be used for?

People often look for funding to cover essentials such as rent, utilities, groceries, transportation, recovery-related expenses, or other everyday obligations while a case is unresolved.

What should I review before signing?

Review the amount offered, the full agreement, how repayment works, any pricing or fees included, and how the arrangement fits with the financial problem you are trying to solve.

How long does the process take?

Timing varies by company, by case complexity, and by how quickly information can be gathered and reviewed. A clearer site should set expectations without making promises it cannot control.
